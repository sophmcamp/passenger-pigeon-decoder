<h1><a href="https://emilysuvada.com/extras/the-pigeon-poem">The Pigeon Poem</a> – Emily Suvada</h1>
My body is a prison for my mind;<br>
A cage of bone and sinew for my soul.<br>
This double-helix is a chain that binds<br>
My will, forcing me under its control.<br>
I live in flesh, in suffering and toil,<br>
Dreaming of higher things, yearning to fly;<br>
To wrench my spirit from this mortal coil<br>
And soar, unfettered, into open sky.<br>
So I will split this helix at its core;<br>
Destroy the genes that formed this ancient cage.<br>
Rebuild this body into something more<br>
Than flesh and blood and misery and rage.<br>
And yet, the laws of physics may prove right;<br>
Dark matter cannot transform into light.
